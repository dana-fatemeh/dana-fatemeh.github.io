About

I am a Ph.D. candidate researching embedded systems security with practical experience building and evaluating hardware attack and defence tools. My work blends experimental platforms (FPGA, SoC), Python tooling, and security analysis to improve system robustness in safety‑critical devices.

Research interests

  • Fault injection & glitching (voltage, clock)
  • Side‑channel analysis and countermeasures
  • Secure hardware/software co‑design for embedded systems
  • Instrumentation and measurement pipelines (FPGA, TRC parsing, visualization)

Recent Experience & Projects

  • Graduate Research Assistant – Vernam Applied Cryptography and Secure Embedded Systems Lab, WPI Sept 2024 – Present

    • Conducted FPGA-based undervolting experiments, revealing static side-channel leakage in secure SoCs and proposing hardware-level countermeasures (Chypnosis, IEEE S&P 2026).
    • Investigated high-frequency localized bit flips in DDR4 DRAM using TRRespass and BlackSmith to model adjacent Rowhammer effects, assessing impact on cryptographic key recovery and LLM token corruption (Rubber Mallet, DRAMSec 2025).
    • Designed fault-tolerant RISC-V and AES cores in Verilog; developed C++/Python assembler replacing faulty instructions with safe alternatives to improve soft-error recovery (Logical Maneuvers, SpaceSec 2025).
    • Implemented on-chip voltage/clock sensors, UART/JTAG interfaces, and TDC-based timing for real-time fault detection.
    • Developed Python test automation for fault injection, power modulation, and data collection across 10,000+ runs.
    • Analyzed FPGA and power distribution network behavior under voltage and frequency stress, correlating physical with digital fault signatures.

  • Teaching Assistant – Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) Jan 2025 – May 2025, Aug 2025 – Dec 2025

    • TA for graduate courses in Digital Signal Processing (DSP) and Cryptography & Security.
    • Prepared and graded assignments and exams; supported MATLAB-based labs and signal analysis projects.
    • Provided constructive feedback on student reports.
